THE FILIPINO'S GUIDE TO
DATA BREACH SURVIVAL

What to do if your personal data has been compromised

RECOGNIZE THE
WARNING SIGNS

  • Unexpected login alerts
  • Strange charges on your bank / e-wallet
  • Phishing emails or texts pretending to be from trusted companies
  • Sudden password reset notification

SECURE YOUR ACCOUNTS
IMMEDIATELY

  • Change all passwords (use strong, unique ones)
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
  • Log out devices you don't recognize
  • Update recovery emails / numbers

REPORT THE BREACH

  • File a complaint with the National Privacy Commission (NPC)
  • Email: complaints@privacy.gov.ph
  • Hotline: (02) 8284-2228
  • Notify your bank, GCash, Maya, or other provider if financial
    info is compromised

MONITOR & PROTECT
YOUR IDENTITY

  • Watch out for phishing scams and suspicious links.
  • Regularly check your bank and e-wallet transactions.
  • If banking info is leaked, consider requesting a credit freeze.
  • Inform your friends and family so they won't fall for scams
    from your hacked accounts.
  • Use strong passwords (mix of letters, numbers, symbols)
  • Don't reuse passwords across accounts
  • Be suspicious of "too good to be true" offers
  • Don't click unknown links in emails or text messages
